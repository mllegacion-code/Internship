About the Host-Training Establishment (HTE)

This section provides an overview of my Host Training Establishment (HTE), the Department of Science and Technology – Philippine Council for Industry, Energy, and Emerging Technology Research and Development (DOST-PCIEERD). It introduces the agency's mission, functions, and the Finance and Administrative Division, where I completed my internship. This section also outlines the objectives of my internship.

About DOST-PCIEERD

The Department of Science and Technology – Philippine Council for Industry, Energy, and Emerging Technology Research and Development (DOST-PCIEERD) is one of the sectoral councils under the Department of Science and Technology (DOST). It is responsible for planning, coordinating, and supporting research and development initiatives that promote innovation and technological advancement in the Philippines.

DOST-PCIEERD funds research projects, develops partnerships among government, academia, and industry, and supports programs that strengthen the country's capabilities in science, engineering, and technology. Through these initiatives, the council contributes to national development by transforming research outputs into technologies, products, and services that benefit society.

Major Divisions

Office of the Executive Director and Deputy Executive Director

Provides overall leadership, strategic direction, and policy oversight for DOST-PCIEERD while ensuring the effective implementation of its programs and operations.

Emerging Technology Development Division

Supports research and innovation in emerging technologies such as artificial intelligence, biotechnology, electronics, and advanced materials.

Energy and Utilities Systems Technology Development Division

Promotes research on sustainable energy systems, utilities, and technologies that improve national energy security and efficiency.

Industry Technology Development Division

Develops technologies and supports innovation that strengthen Philippine industries, manufacturing, and industrial competitiveness.

Policy Coordination and Monitoring Division

Monitors research projects, evaluates program performance, and supports policy planning and implementation across the Council.

Research Information and Technology Transfer Division

Facilitates knowledge dissemination, technology commercialization, and partnerships among government, industry, and academia.

Finance and Administrative Division

Manages financial resources, accounting, budgeting, procurement, human resources, and administrative support services of the Council.

Human Resources and Institution Development Division

Oversees workforce development, organizational learning, employee welfare, and institutional capability-building initiatives.

Finance and Administrative Division

Finance and Administrative Division (FAD) is the division responsible for ensuring the efficient management of the council's financial resources and administrative operations.

The division oversees accounting, budgeting, financial reporting, procurement, records management, human resources, and other administrative services that support the daily operations of DOST-PCIEERD.

Additional information regarding the programs, services, organizational structure, and initiatives of DOST-PCIEERD can be found on its official website.

Internship Objectives

The internship was designed to provide practical exposure to the operations of a government agency while developing technical, analytical, and professional competencies. It emphasized the application of Microsoft Excel for financial data management, validation, reconciliation, and reporting, highlighting the importance of spreadsheets as a fundamental tool in finance and data analysis. The internship also introduced student-trainee to the integration of accounting principles and data analysis in supporting accurate financial reporting, efficient data processing, and informed decision-making within a professional work environment.
